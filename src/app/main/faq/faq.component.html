Let's settle some questions...

Let's face facts, we all know that the temperature of the seas around Britain can be cold, but the good news is that it can reach a toasy 20°C in the summer months - and this is the sort of temperature that some can tolerate without a wetsuit. However, away from the summer, sea temperatures can get much colder and you will definitaly need a wetsuit to protect you from the elements if you want to be a year-round snorkeller...

Snorkelling, like any outdoor activity, comes with risks - that's part of the reason for doing it. But there are many common-sense things we can do to enjoy the marine enviornment safely, but number one on our list will always be: It's so important that you have someone to look out for you, even if they stay on the shore.

There is so much to see underwater that describing it here could never do it justice. And you won't see everything every time you go in the water - what you see can be about selecting the right site at the right time, along with a liberal helping of luck! That said, the marine environment around our coasts are internationally recognised by marine biologists for their diversity, and our advice is: Never go anywhere without your mask and snorkel ... you never know what waiting for you!
